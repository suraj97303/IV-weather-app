Mumbai likely to receive rainfall between November 16 to 19

The brief spell of winter-like chill weather has passed. Maharashtra and the adjoining states are now set to witness some wet days, with thunder and lightning.According to weathermen, Mumbai is likely to receive rainfall between November 16 and November 19. This will be under the influence of the likely formation of a low-pressure system in the Bay of Bengal and its west-northwestward movement to mainland India.

Rain activity is also expected from today, November 15 to November 19 in Konkan, Goa, Madhya (central) Maharashtra, Marathwada region and farther, up to South Gujarat.Rajesh Kapadia from Vagaries of Weather, a private weather blog said: “In Lonavala and Mahabaleshwar, rains are expected between November 15 to November 19. Mumbai is to receive cumulative rainfall of 10-15 mms between November 16 to November 19 whereas Pune is likely to receive cumulative rains of 30-45 between November 16 to November 19. Similarly, Goa is expected to receive Cumulative rains of 60-75 mms between November 15 to November 19.”

“Low low-pressure area is over the Central part of the Andaman Sea associated cyclonic circulation is extending up to 5.8 km above mean sea level. It is expected to move in the West-Northwest direction and concentrate into a depression over Southeast Bay of Bengal by November 15,” according to experts from Skymet Weather, a private weather agency.

The IMD had, on Saturday, issued a ‘yellow’ alert over most districts of southern Konkan, Madhya Maharashtra and Marathwada.
